Sonified steering commands from Contingency Model Predictive Control





Two plans are being maintained simulataneously: a nominal and an emergency plan.
The sound signals contain the Fourier Tranformed frequency content of each new
new plan as its published by the path-planning controller. The plans are initially
different, but they converge as the emergency occurs.

Follow the link below to see Contingency Planning in action:
Contingency Planning for Pedestrian Avoidance

It didn't turn out very musical, but very fun to learn how to pipe so much
data (1560 individual oscillators!) into a javascript audio context.
Now that I've worked through the coding implementation details, I hope to have
more time in future assignments to think about composition.
